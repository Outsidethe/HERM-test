KITCHEN

Kitchen Space

Kitchens see grease, heat and constant cleaning, so durability is just as important as colour choice. Here's how to plan a kitchen repaint that looks sharp and holds up to everyday cooking life.

DESIGN & PREP TIPS
01

Pick a wipeable, grease-resistant finish — gloss or satin emulsions clean far more easily than flat matte paints near hobs and counters.

02

Warm, appetising tones (terracotta, mustard, cream) work well with natural materials like wood and stone.

03

Tape off and protect appliances, extractor hoods and tiling before you start; grease film can stop new paint from bonding properly.

04

Use a stain-blocking primer over any smoke or oil marks so they don't bleed through your new topcoat.
